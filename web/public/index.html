A machine-learning forecast of the 2026 World Cup, built entirely from public data. Each team gets a strength rating from three things — its national-team Elo, the market value of its squad, and how well its players are playing for their clubs. A model turns those into odds for every match, then plays the rest of the tournament out 100,000 times; the percentages below are how often each team reaches each stage.

It updates itself: after every match the new result feeds back in, the model re-runs, and this page refreshes — so the forecast always reflects what's happened so far.

Betting odds are never an input — the model never sees the market. They're used only as a scorecard afterwards: lining the forecast up against the bookmakers' closing odds is how we check it's any good.
