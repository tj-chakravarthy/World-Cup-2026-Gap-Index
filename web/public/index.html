My machine-learning forecast of the 2026 World Cup, built from public data (Code & method on GitHub).

Each team gets a strength rating from its national-team Elo (computed from past international results) and the market value of its squad (Transfermarkt). My model turns those into odds for every match, then plays the rest of the tournament out 100 000 times; the percentages below are how often each team reaches each stage. (I also rate players from club stats — FBref and Understat — but it didn't improve the forecast, so the live model leaves it out.)

Model updates itself: after every match the new result feeds back in, the model re-runs, and this page refreshes — so the forecast always reflects what's happened so far.

Betting odds are never an input — the ML never uses them. On the four held-out backtest tournaments (Euro 2020, WC 2022, Euro 2024, Copa 2024) my model scores 0.589 Brier, just behind Pinnacle's closing odds at 0.573 — measured on the same matches.

Gap Index — talent vs result

The project's namesake. Each squad gets a talent rating from its market value and club form; that predicts expected points per game. The gap is the residual — what a team did beyond its talent — and the residual is the story. Across five completed tournaments talent explains about a third of results (R²≈·); the bands are wide because three group games is a tiny sample. Over to the right, under to the left.

Player ratings

I rate every player in the 48 squads from club stats — FBref minutes and Understat expected goals turned into a per-90 VAEP estimate, blended with market value into a 0–100 score. It didn't move the match forecast (see Model), so the live model leaves it out — but the ratings stand on their own.
